Building better robot learning systems#

AI engineer exploring how to design and build scalable robot learning systems,
with a current focus on Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, simulation benchmarking,
and evaluation infrastructure for robot policies.

This site documents notes, experiments, and ideas encountered while exploring how robot foundation models might be built in practice.

What I’m exploring#

Scaling has transformed fields like natural language processing and computer vision.

Robotics is beginning to follow a similar direction with Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, which aim to unify perception, language grounding, and control.

However, building useful robot learning systems involves more than training models.

Some questions I am currently interested in include:

  • How should VLA development pipelines be structured?
  • What makes a reliable simulation benchmarking system?
  • What are the core components of a scalable robot learning stack?
  • How can research ideas be translated into practical engineering workflows?

About this site#

This site is a personal notebook for organizing ideas encountered while exploring robot learning.

Most posts are written while reading papers, experimenting with open-source tools, or thinking through system design questions, rather than after everything is fully figured out.

The goal is to capture:

  • insights from research papers
  • lessons from small experiments and prototypes
  • conceptual frameworks for reasoning about robot learning systems

All notes are based on publicly available papers, open-source projects, and personal experiments.
