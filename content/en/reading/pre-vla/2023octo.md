**Octo: An Open-Source Generalist Robot Policy**

Octo is a transformer-based diffusion policy trained on 800k robot trajectories from the OXE dataset. Octo supports flexible observation and action spaces, efficient finetuning, and is fully open source. It’s a big encoder-decoder transformer with small encoders (T5 and CNN) at the input and a small action head (MLP) at the output. Uses diffusion action decoding for max expressiveness

**Architecture**

The architecture depends on three parts:

- **Input tokenizers**: takes as input language instruction **l**, goals **g** and observation sequences **o1,...,oh** and transforms them into tokens **tl,tg,to**
  - **Language inputs** are tokenized, then passed through a pretrained transformer (**t5-base 111M**) that produces a sequence of language embedding tokens. Then adding a learnable position embedding p resulting in **Tt**
  - **Image observations and goals** are passed through a shallow convolution stack, then split into a sequence of flattened patches. Then adding a learnable position embedding p resulting in **To,1 To,2 … .**
- **Transformer backbone**: Once the above input (language, images) is passed to a unified task space through a projection layer (probably MLP), they are then processed by a encoder-decoder transformer that processes the tokens and produces embeddings **el,eg,eo** = T(**Tl,Tg,To**)
- **Readout + Head**: **R(e)** is applied to the readout tokens Tr,t and produce the desired outputs i.e, actions **a**
  - The diffusion action head is characterized by a 3-layer MLP with a hidden dimension of 256, residual connections, and layer normalization
- Pretrained weights are available for **Octo-Small** (27M parameters) and **Octo-Base** (93M parameters)
